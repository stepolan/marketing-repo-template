## Draft

Most ultra-runners are undereating carbs by 40 grams an hour.  The old rule was 60 grams per hour.  The research has moved.  For efforts over three hours, trained athletes can absorb and oxidize 90-120 grams per hour with the right carbohydrate ratios.

The difference is not subtle.  The athlete fueling at 90+ consistently outperforms the athlete fueling at 60 in the final third of any long race.  You see it in heart rate, perceived effort, and pace.

The catch is that you cannot flip a switch on race day.  Gut absorption is trainable, and it takes four to six weeks of progressive fueling during long workouts.

I wrote about what changed in the research and what it means in practice.  Link in the first comment.

What is your target intake on long efforts?  I am curious how people are actually fueling.
